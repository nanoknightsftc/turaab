About This Skill

Whether it's a PC that won't boot, a robot mechanism that's failing during competition, or finding the right components within a tight budget, I approach every challenge methodically. My problem-solving ability comes from combining technical knowledge with analytical thinking and staying calm under pressure.

Real Problem-Solving Examples

PC Won't Boot After Upgrade

Problem: Client's PC wouldn't POST after installing a new graphics card.

Approach: Systematically tested each component, checked power connections, and verified PSU wattage.

Solution: Discovered the PSU was underpowered for the new GPU. Upgraded to appropriate PSU and system worked perfectly.

Random System Crashes During Gaming

Problem: System would crash randomly during intensive games but work fine otherwise.

Approach: Monitored temperatures, ran stress tests, checked event logs, and tested RAM.

Solution: Found CPU was overheating under load due to improperly mounted cooler. Reseated cooler with fresh thermal paste.

Robot Mechanism Failing During Match

Problem: Robot's lifting mechanism would intermittently fail during FTC competition matches.

Approach: Quick diagnosis between matches, checked connections, motor stalls, and weight distribution.

Solution: Identified loose wire connection causing power loss. Secured connection and reinforced for remaining matches.

Budget Build Performance Issues

Problem: Client had $600 budget but wanted high gaming performance.

Approach: Researched price-to-performance ratios, identified bottlenecks, and prioritized components.

Solution: Allocated more budget to GPU, chose cost-effective CPU/motherboard combo, and planned upgrade path. Client got 60+ FPS in target games.

My Problem-Solving Approach

1. Define the Problem

Clearly identify what's wrong, when it happens, and what the symptoms are.

2. Gather Information

Collect all relevant data, error messages, symptoms, and context.

3. Form Hypotheses

Based on symptoms, develop potential explanations for the problem.

4. Test Systematically

Test each hypothesis methodically, changing one variable at a time.

5. Isolate the Cause

Use process of elimination to narrow down to the root cause.

6. Implement Solution

Apply the fix, then verify it resolves the issue completely.
